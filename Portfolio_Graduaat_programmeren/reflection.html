Personal reflection group project WPL2

The project was very educational but challenging because we had to learn many new things first.
The tasks were clearly formulated, and we knew what we needed to achieve. I had a reasonable amount of
support from my team members and lecturers. My team members picked up on things when I had no idea or
when things became too overwhelming to think clearly. The lecturers were also a very good source of
knowledge because they helped us write proper controllers with safe queries. They also gave good tips
that helped us understand how to approach things in terms of code or even find mistakes that we didn't see.

In my opinion, the most important thing I learned in this project is how to make a connection between
a front-end and a back-end. Additionally, understanding how controllers and classes work together was
extremely educational and will be beneficial for future projects.

Critical thinking improved through this project because I became better at asking questions and using
the right resources like ChatGPT. As I mentioned earlier, the lecturers were also a good source of
information. Creativity, in my personal opinion, has remained the same. The only area where I became
a bit more creative was in adapting code to work for other, similar purposes. Independence
consistently increased during the project because, since sprint 3, we asked fewer questions and found
our own solutions to problems. This ultimately led us to complete the project, although it still had
its bugs and features that needed addressing.

What I would change about our collaboration is that we should have done more after class to get more
done and, by extension, deliver better quality.

I would add and improve certain features to our controllers, such as an additional feature that
changes values from ordered to canceled. With the knowledge I have now, I could also complete
everything faster, allowing more time for extra features.

Regarding the factors of the X-factor, I possess aspects of several such as collaboration, empathy,
and being (multi)disciplinary, but entrepreneurial & innovative still needs a lot of work.
Collaboration took the forefront because we had to create a group project over several months.
This allowed me to dust off skills like communication and mediation, but also to pay attention to
my tendency to be condescending. I also focused on not always saying yes to everything, so I could
occasionally take care of myself.

Empathy is one of the two parts I definitely possess. I can put myself in someone else's shoes
without it negatively affecting me. This allows me to immerse myself in someone's story and
transform it into an idea, for example, a website layout that aligns with the client's vision.
Passion is also present, but to a lesser extent. I was passionate about the group project and coding,
but this waned a bit over the course of the project.

(Multi)disciplinary: I have certain skills that I can use in different situations, such as
listening to what people want, being flexible, and self-discipline. Listening to the client
(lecturers in this case) but also listening to your team, being flexible to do more than one thing
and thus being useful in multiple languages, and self-discipline to finish what I start but also
knowing when I can't continue because I've reached my limit.

ondernemen and innovatief: I also improved in taking initiative/entrepreneurship because I
took on the role of scrummaster. Additionally, if no one took on a difficult task, I took the leap
of faith and took on these tasks to learn and see how I could do things like creating controllers.
